The gap

Interest comes in. Most of it leaves before you ever hear from it.

People arrive on your site already interested. But the main thing you ask them to do is fill out a full application. For a three-year, life-changing decision, almost nobody is ready for that on the first visit. So the ones who are still thinking about it quietly leave, and you never get the chance to talk to them.

No easy way to raise a hand

The only real next step is a full application. There is no simple "send me the details" option for someone who is interested but not ready to commit today.

No follow-up to the undecided

The people who are curious but on the fence get no guide, no answers, and no gentle follow-up. They are left to decide entirely on their own, and most drift away.

A bigger audience never hears of you

Plenty of people would enroll if they knew this career existed. They are not searching for it yet, so they never find you, and that demand goes uncaptured.

The solution

Make it easy to say "tell me more," then guide them to enroll.

Nothing here touches what is already working. It adds the missing middle, the part that turns interest into a conversation and a conversation into an enrollment.

1An easy first step

A simple "request the program guide / talk to admissions" option, front and center, instead of asking strangers to apply cold. Far more people will raise their hand.

2Steady, helpful follow-up

Once someone raises a hand, a clear sequence answers their real questions over the weeks they take to decide: the guide, the founder's story, licensing, financial aid and GI Bill, an invite to visit.

3Reach the right new people

Get in front of the career-changers who would love this path but have never heard the word, with simple education-led campaigns, so the audience grows instead of staying flat.

4See what is actually working

Know which efforts bring in real inquiries and enrollments, so you put money and time only into what pays off.

Already checked

A few specific things I already spotted

No access needed, just a look at your live site. Small, fixable items, and the kind of thing we clean up first so the numbers are trustworthy before any spend.

Your analytics is double-firing

The site runs two Google Analytics properties and two Tag Manager containers at once, which usually means duplicated or conflicting numbers. It is hard to trust a report built on that.

Google is pointed at a dead map of your site

Your robots file sends search engines to a sitemap address that now returns an error, a leftover from an SEO plugin change. Quick fix, but it slows how cleanly you get crawled.

Your program pages have no structured data

Nothing tells Google these are accredited degree programs, so you get a plainer search listing than you have earned.

The pages are heavier than they need to be

The homepage alone sends about 600KB before images. Your server is quick; the weight is on the page itself, which drags load time on phones.

The downside of waiting

The same interest keeps arriving, and keeps leaving.

The gap is not a one-time loss. Every cohort it stays open, the same thing happens: interested people show up, find no easy next step, and go. On a small cohort, a couple of lost students is a big share of the class, and it repeats every term.
