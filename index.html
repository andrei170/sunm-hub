What is leaking

Four gaps between your traffic and your enrollments

The first click asks for too much

The main call to action sends a cold visitor straight into a full application instead of an easy "request information" step. Most people researching a three-year, high-cost decision are not ready to apply on day one, so they leave.

Nothing captures the researcher

There is no simple guide or fit-check to opt into. The large group who are curious but undecided have no low-commitment next step, so you never get the chance to follow up.

You cannot see what is working

The tracking that would prove which sources, pages, and ads produce inquiries could not be confirmed. Without it, spend cannot be measured or scaled with confidence.

The bigger market does not know you exist

Almost no one searches "naprapathic medicine" because they have never heard the word. The career-changers who would enroll are reachable, but only through education-led outreach you are not yet running at scale.

The plan

Turn the category you own into measured enrollments

Built to start small and prove itself. The first moves are low-cost and high-proof, then we scale what the data rewards.

01

Confirm and complete tracking

Analytics, pixel, conversion events on every inquiry and call. The prerequisite for spending a dollar with confidence.

02

A soft request-info funnel

An easy "request the program guide / talk to admissions" step above the application, plus a lead magnet for the undecided. A clean, fast landing page for traffic to hit.

03

Inquiry-to-application nurture

An email and text sequence built for a months-long decision: program guide, founder story, licensure clarity, financial aid and GI Bill, tour invite.

04

The awareness engine

Education-led campaigns on Meta and YouTube to reach career-changers who do not know naprapathy yet, plus adjacent-intent search and a GI Bill / veteran push, all measured on cost per inquiry.

Plus a quick technical cleanup (consolidate duplicate pages, add program schema, retire stale pages, speed pass) so the ranking you already hold compounds. All of it inside higher-ed advertising rules: no income or job-placement promises, outcomes only from your own published data.

Questions

Good to know

Do we have to rebuild the whole website?

No. The site has real strengths and a number-one ranking worth protecting. The work is surgical: a soft request-info step, a fast landing page for paid traffic, tracking, nurture, and a cleanup. We keep what is working.

How do we start small?

We begin with the low-cost, high-proof pieces: tracking plus the request-info funnel and one landing page. That alone makes the leak visible and measurable. Everything else scales from there once the data shows what is working.

Is this compliant for an accredited institution?

Yes. Everything is built inside higher-ed advertising rules: no income or job-placement guarantees, accreditation stated precisely, and outcome claims only from your own published DEAC and institutional data.

Who runs it after launch?

Your choice. I can hand it off with training and SOPs, or run the campaigns and nurture on a month-to-month basis. A structure tied to enrolled students is on the table too.
